About

The work beneath the work.

I trained as a psychotherapist. I spent years working at the level most people never go — sitting with high-performers in the space beneath the persona, beneath the performance, beneath the story they've been telling themselves about who they are.

What I kept seeing, over and over, was this:

The most capable people in the room were often the loneliest in their own inner lives.

Not because they lacked intelligence or self-awareness. They had plenty of both. But because the very capacities that made them successful — the relentless discipline, the ability to compartmentalize, the practiced ease of never showing strain — were also what kept them from the person they were quietly becoming.

I built Self Discovery Atlas for them. For you.

This isn't therapy. And it isn't coaching.

Therapy looks backward to heal. Coaching looks forward to perform. What I do lives in a different register entirely.

I work with the underlying structure — the belief architecture, the identity constructs, the unconscious agreements you've made about what you're allowed to want, who you're allowed to be, and how much is enough.

When that structure changes, everything built on top of it changes. Not gradually. Structurally.

That's the work.

The structure of this work
